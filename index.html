Round One: Build an MVP of Your Idea

Challenge Overview

This round challenges you to take your idea or problem and create a Minimum Viable Product (MVP)—the simplest, most essential version that addresses the core problem or demonstrates the primary value of your idea.

Evaluation Criteria

Your submission will be judged on the following:

1. Idea Feasibility (Weightage: 20%)

Explain and validate your idea by answering the following:

  • Validation: How did you validate your idea or problem? (e.g., user research, market analysis, competitor analysis).
  • Problem Importance: Why is this problem worth solving? Is it significant enough to have a commercial outcome?
  • Business Model: What is your revenue model? How will you monetize the solution?

2. Technical Implementation (Weightage: 80%)

Demonstrate your technical progress:

  • MVP Features: What have you built so far? It could be a website, an app, a working algorithm, or a partial solution.
  • Critical Focus: Highlight the part of your idea you’ve solved, especially the part you believe is most important or impactful.
  • Challenges: Mention any technical challenges you faced and how you addressed them.

Deliverables

We expect the following deliverables as part of your submission:

  • GitHub Repository: A link to your code repository with a README file that includes clear instructions on how to run the code.
  • Documentation: A document explaining your idea, how real the problem is, and your technical implementation. This can be in the form of a PDF, Google Doc, or any other shareable format.

Flexible Submission Formats

These are not rigid requirements. You have the flexibility to present your idea or problem through a Business Model Canvas (BMC) or a Pitch Deck instead. Use these formats to outline your idea, the problem you’re solving, and your plan for turning it into a commercial success. The goal is to clearly communicate the value of your idea and its feasibility.

Important Notes

  • You don’t need a perfect solution, but your submission should demonstrate significant progress on solving the most critical part of the problem.
  • Include visuals (e.g., screenshots, prototypes) or short demonstrations of your work, such as Google Forms you circulated and interviews you conducted with people who need this solution.
  • Team Size: The maximum team size allowed is 2 members.
  • Submission Deadline: The final submission date is 10 Feb.
